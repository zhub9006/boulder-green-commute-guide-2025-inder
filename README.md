# Boulder Green Commute Guide 🌿🚲

A practical guide to sustainable commuting options in Boulder, Colorado — helping residents choose greener travel choices for their daily commutes.

## About This Guide

This repository shares real-world commute analyses for Boulder residents considering walking or biking as sustainable alternatives to driving. Every route is based on actual OpenStreetMap routing data.

### Walking Route Details

**Total Distance:** 4.0 km  
**Est. Duration:** ~48 minutes at a comfortable walking pace  
**Terrain:** Mostly flat with gentle grades through central Boulder

**Turn-by-Turn Directions:**
1. Head south on **Oak Avenue** from your starting point
2. Continue onto **19th Street** — the main north-south corridor through the university area
3. Turn onto **20th St./20th Street** — a quiet residential-friendly street
4. Continue on **20th Street** as it winds toward downtown
5. Merge onto **Pearl Street** — Boulder's vibrant downtown main street
6. Continue on **18th Street** through the heart of downtown
7. Turn onto **Canyon Boulevard** — a major Boulder artery with wide sidewalks
8. Arrive at **1800 Broadway** near the Canyon Boulevard intersection

**Walking Highlights:**
- Passes through Boulder's historic Pearl Street Mall area
- See public art, cafes, and local shops along the way
- Well-maintained sidewalks throughout the route
- Great opportunity to explore downtown Boulder

### Biking Route Details

**Total Distance:** 4.0 km  
**Est. Duration:** ~16 minutes at a moderate biking pace  
**Terrain:** Mostly flat; Boulder is famously bike-friendly

**Turn-by-Turn Directions:**
1. Head south on **Oak Avenue** from your starting point
2. Continue onto **19th Street** — use the designated bike lanes
3. Turn onto **20th St./20th Street** — bike-friendly streets with low traffic
4. Continue on **20th Street** toward downtown
5. Merge onto **Pearl Street** — use the bike lane alongside pedestrian traffic
6. Continue on **18th Street** through downtown
7. Turn onto **Canyon Boulevard** — wide bike path runs alongside the road
8. Arrive at **1800 Broadway** near the Canyon Boulevard intersection

**Biking Highlights:**
- Boulder has extensive bike infrastructure and bike lanes
- Canyon Boulevard has a dedicated multi-use path
- Flat terrain through much of the route
- Park at ample bike racks downtown

## Why Commute Green in Boulder?

- 🏔️ **Flat terrain** — Boulder's elevation makes cycling easier than you'd think
- 🚲 **Bike infrastructure** — Over 200 miles of bike paths and lanes
- 🌡️ **300+ days of sunshine** — Great weather for walking and biking year-round
- 🏙️ **Compact downtown** — Pearl Street and surrounding areas are walkable
- 🌱 **Environmental impact** — Every car trip replaced saves ~0.4 kg CO₂

## Tips for Boulder Commuters

### For Walkers:
- Wear comfortable shoes — the route is ~4 km one way
- Carry water, especially in Boulder's dry climate
- Use pedestrian crosswalks on Canyon Boulevard — it's a busy road
- Enjoy the Pearl Street Mall area on your lunch break

### For Cyclists:
- Use bike lights — Colorado law requires them at night
- Lock your bike securely — downtown Boulder has theft issues
- Check the [Boulder Bike Master Plan](https://bouldercolorado.gov/transportation/bike-program) for route updates
- Consider a commuter bike with fenders for year-round riding
